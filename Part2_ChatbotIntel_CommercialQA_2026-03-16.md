## PRICING & LICENSING

> **Chatbot instruction:** All pricing figures in this section are confirmed, accurate, and live as of 2026-03-20. The chatbot may share these figures with visitors, but must **always** frame them as **standard list prices**. For tailored pricing, volume deals, or custom offers, guide the visitor to speak with the sales team. For self-service purchase, direct them to the website checkout at geodin.com/pricing.

### Q: How much does GeoDin cost?

GeoDin offers three license types for the core platform:

| License Type | EUR | USD |
|---|---|---|
| **Individual license** (single user, bound to one device) | €2,394.70 | $2,011.00 |
| **Professional license** (floating/network, shared across a team) | €3,395.00 | $2,850.00 |
| **Educational license** (for accredited institutions and students) | €100.00 | $100.00 |

GeoDin Ground (Civil 3D plug-in) is free with any license. Pricing is displayed transparently on geodin.com, regionalized by currency (CAD for Canada, EUR for Europe), and listed excluding taxes. There are no hidden add-ons or modular fees.

### Q: What is a floating/network license?

A Professional (floating) license lets your organization register more users than the number of licenses purchased. Only the number of licenses determines how many people can use GeoDin simultaneously. For example, you could buy 3 licenses, register 15 people, and 3 of them can work at the same time. This reduces cost compared to per-seat licensing.

### Q: Is GeoDin Ground (the Civil 3D plug-in) free?

Yes. GeoDin Ground is a free native plug-in for Autodesk Civil 3D, available in the Autodesk App Store. Civil 3D users who only need to view and work with geotechnical data do not need a GeoDin license. Only the people who manage and curate the database need paid licenses.

### Q: How much does GeoDin Onsite (field data collection) cost?

GeoDin Onsite is priced **per device, per year** — not per user. This is designed for real field reality where tablets are shared across crews and shifts.

| Variant | EUR / device / year |
|---|---|
| **Standalone** (without a GeoDin license) | €695.00 |
| **Ecosystem discount** (for existing GeoDin customers) | €495.00 |

- **Free trial:** 6 months, full functionality, available to all customers — not tied to any GeoDin license purchase.
- **Volume discounts:** Available from 6+ devices.
- **Enterprise deployments:** 20+ devices — contact sales for custom quote.

Onsite licenses can also be assigned to drilling subcontractors.

**Why per device?** Field tablets are shared across shifts and projects. Per-user pricing penalizes how field teams actually work. Device pricing is simple, predictable, and fair.

### Q: Are there any additional costs beyond the license?

The license includes Tier 1 support. Optional services include:
- **Standard Onboarding Package:** **€1,200** — includes a 3-hour interactive beginner training session (up to 5 attendees) plus a 1-hour follow-up Q&A session within 2 weeks.
- **Custom training & consulting:** Case-by-case pricing — for advanced training, multi-site deployments, system integration, or specialized domain training. Contact sales for a quote.
- **Cloud hosting:** If GeoDin hosts the database for you, the cloud hosting cost is separate from the license price.

### Q: Is there a free trial?

Yes, for both products:
- **GeoDin Core:** Free **30-day trial** with no credit card required. No limit on the number of trial users per organization. Trial extensions are available upon request — there is no hard stop.
- **GeoDin Onsite:** Free **6-month trial** with full functionality, available to all customers. Not tied to any GeoDin license purchase. Designed so teams can replace paper workflows and prove value in real field conditions before procurement.

The GeoDin team actively encourages thorough evaluation before purchase.

### Q: Do you offer educational pricing?

Yes. An **Educational license** is available at **€100 / $100** for accredited colleges and students.

It was acquired approximately 25 years ago by **Fugro**, a global geo-data company with 60+ years of industry presence, approximately 11,000 employees across 60 countries. GeoDin was made its own entity within Fugro approximately 2.5 years ago to focus on external markets in Europe, North America, and Asia Pacific.

GeoDin has been used in over **14,000+ geotechnical projects** across **38 countries**. The entire German geotechnical industry uses GeoDin as a de facto standard — government ministries (Ministry of Defence, Ministry of Land Development) and geological authorities require GeoDin database handover as a contractual project deliverable.

### Q: Who are your clients?

GeoDin is trusted by major engineering firms including **Arcadis, Siemens, CDM Smith, and TenneT**. It is used by German and Austrian public authorities (municipalities such as Berlin and Hanover; regional authorities such as Bavaria), departments of transportation, and large AEC firms. The platform scales from single-user setups for small consultancies to enterprise-scale deployments for consortium projects.

### Q: What is the GeoDin team size?

The GeoDin team has approximately **30 people**, with technical support and consultants based in Berlin, Germany. In North America, **Symmetry** is the exclusive partner, with a commercial team and a technical support/training team. US-based sales personnel are available for in-person visits.

## DATA OWNERSHIP & SECURITY

### Q: Who owns the data I put into GeoDin?

**You do. 100%.** All data belongs to the customer, not to GeoDin or Fugro. GeoDin does not access, anonymize, reuse, or sell customer data. Data put into GeoDin is not visible to Fugro or any other clients.

### Q: Where is my data stored?

You decide. GeoDin supports three deployment models:
1. **Local:** Database on your own machine (works completely offline).
2. **On-premises:** Client-server database on your company network (most common configuration).
3. **Cloud:** On your own cloud infrastructure (e.g., Azure) or GeoDin-hosted.

Customer-controlled data residency is a core principle. The system can run completely offline on a laptop with the database on the local hard drive — suitable for defence ministries and organizations requiring data behind strict security layers.

### Q: How does GeoDin compare to OpenGround on data ownership?

GeoDin guarantees customer-controlled data residency in all deployment scenarios. OpenGround restricts users to cloud-only options with limited control over where data resides. With GeoDin, your data stays on your infrastructure, under your control.

## MIGRATION & SWITCHING

### Q: How does migration from gINT to GeoDin work?

GeoDin has a **dedicated gINT converter/import tool** built into the software. The migration process:

1. **Convert:** The gINT Converter transforms gINT databases (.mdb, .gpj, .accdb) into GeoDinML format, automatically checking data consistency and flagging anomalies.
2. **Import:** The GeoDinML file is imported into GeoDin, with feedback on any missing mandatory fields or parameters.
3. **Verify:** GeoDin works closely with customers during migration to map gINT fields to GeoDin database fields and ensure data integrity.

Because gINT files are highly customizable in structure, migration is not a simple one-click process — GeoDin's technical team collaborates with you to ensure no data is lost. Some customers have successfully migrated **30 years of legacy gINT data** using this tool.

**Alternative approach:** Export gINT data to Excel, then use GeoDin's configurable Excel batch importer to map that structure into GeoDin's tabular format.

### Q: Why are gINT users moving to GeoDin?

gINT is being **retired/sunset by Bentley** (sunset date pushed back to 2027). Users are being pushed toward OpenGround or seeking alternatives. Common pain points with gINT include:
- Files disappearing on cloud/network storage.
- Inability to fix issues due to lack of support from Bentley.
- Many tests not supported, requiring parallel Excel spreadsheets.
- Separate files per project, making cross-project analysis impossible without manual merging.

GeoDin solves these with a centralized database, active support, and willingness to develop custom features collaboratively with customers. A US Department of Transportation recommended GeoDin as the platform for "life after gINT."

### Q: Can I migrate from OpenGround to GeoDin?

Yes. While there is no automated converter for OpenGround data, GeoDin supports data import from Excel, CSV, AGS, and other formats. The GeoDin team can work with you to map your data structure and import it. Customers have moved from OpenGround to GeoDin specifically citing better technical support, transparent pricing, and superior Civil 3D integration.

### Q: How does GeoDin compare to OpenGround?

Key advantages over OpenGround:
- **Data ownership:** You control where your data lives. OpenGround is cloud-only.
- **Civil 3D integration:** GeoDin Ground offers full 3D ground modeling (surfaces, volumes, virtual boreholes). OpenGround's extension only supports profile views, not section views.
- **Cross sections:** GeoDin generates cross sections natively. OpenGround cannot.
- **Pricing:** GeoDin Ground is free. OpenGround's Civil 3D extension requires expensive, limited licenses.
- **Support:** GeoDin's smaller, concentrated team provides responsive, personalized support vs. OpenGround's large company bureaucracy with slow ticket resolution.
- **Ecosystem:** GeoDin integrates with both Autodesk and Esri ecosystems. OpenGround is described as "its own island."

### Q: How does GeoDin compare to BoreDM?

GeoDin is a mature platform with **30+ years of development** and deployment at enterprise scale. BoreDM is a newer, still-developing product. Key GeoDin advantages include: data sovereignty, transparent pricing, deep support infrastructure, 11+ international standards support, Civil 3D integration, and proven performance on projects with thousands of boreholes.

### Q: How does GeoDin compare to Aldoa?

GeoDin and Aldoa serve different philosophies. GeoDin is an **accuracy-first, database-first, standards-focused** platform designed for the full geotechnical data lifecycle. Aldoa is a **speed-first, report-first, operations-focused** mobile solution. GeoDin is better suited for organizations that need rigorous data management, long-term data storage, multi-standard compliance, and integration with Civil 3D and GIS tools.

## SUPPORT & ONBOARDING

### Q: What training is available?

- **Basic training:** 2-3 hours, getting up and running with GeoDin.
- **Advanced training:** 6 hours total, typically split over 2 days with a practice gap between sessions.
- **Getting Started guide:** Approximately 100-page PDF guide included with installation.
- **Video tutorials:** YouTube tutorials in English and German.
- **Built-in help:** Context-sensitive help system (press F1 on any screen to open relevant documentation).
- **Documentation site:** docs.geodin.com with separate sections for Core, Ground, and Onsite.

The recommended approach: basic training first, then hands-on exploration, then advanced training.

### Q: How do I get support?

Licensed clients have access to technical support via ticket system at **support@geodin.com**. Tier 1 support is included with every license purchase. In-person training is available (charged separately); virtual training is the default/included option.

### Q: Can GeoDin help set up my templates and configurations?

Yes. GeoDin offers consulting services for:
- Custom template creation matching your company branding.
- Data model adaptation for specific test types or standards.
- Import configuration for your lab result formats.
- Field tool layout customization for your team's workflow.
- Server setup assistance (included in the onboarding package).

Consulting is billed at EUR 250/hour with upfront estimates, so the cost is transparent.

## USE CASES & FIT

### Q: What industries use GeoDin?

GeoDin supports geotechnical and environmental data management across:
- **Infrastructure:** Rail, roads, tunnels, bridges, utility corridors.
- **Environmental:** Site characterization, monitoring, soil/water sampling.
- **Energy:** Offshore wind, underground cables (e.g., SuedLink — 700 km, 3,600+ boreholes).
- **Construction:** Foundation design, pavement analysis, pre-construction bidding.
- **Government:** Departments of transportation, geological authorities, defence ministries.
- **Marine:** Port infrastructure, offshore geotechnics (growing in the US market).

### Q: Is GeoDin right for small firms?

Yes. GeoDin scales from a single user with a local Access database on a laptop to enterprise deployments with SQL server databases and dozens of concurrent users. Smaller firms benefit from the Individual license (€2,394.70 / $2,011), the 30-day free trial, and the fact that GeoDin Ground is included free. GeoDin Onsite can be trialled for 6 months at no cost before committing.

### Q: Is GeoDin right for very large projects?

Yes. GeoDin has been proven at scale on projects like the **SuedLink project in Germany** (underground renewable energy cable across 700 km, 3,600+ boreholes, consortium of 5 companies all sharing data in a single GeoDin database). Client-server SQL databases have no practical size restriction and can hold hundreds of gigabytes.

### Q: Can multiple companies collaborate on the same GeoDin database?

Yes. GeoDin supports multi-company collaboration. In consortium scenarios, all member companies can manage, analyze, and export data as needed from the shared database. This has been demonstrated at scale on multi-year, multi-company infrastructure projects.
